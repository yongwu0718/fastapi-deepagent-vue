## ABSTRACT

In this investigation, corrosion studies were conducted on dissimilar friction stir spot welded AZ31 Mg–C27200 Cu joints using pitting corrosion tests. Mechanical characteristics of the joints were also evaluated. By conducting tensile testing, the tensile shear failure load value of AZ31 Mg–C27200 Cu joints were 62.36% of the value of similar AZ31 Mg–AZ31 Mg joints and 51.9% of the values of C27200 Cu–C27200 Cu joints, respectively. The joint interface hardness of the dissimilar combination such as AZ31 Mg–C27200 Cu joints were 22.03% and 35.84% greater than the interface hardness values of similar AZ31 Mg–AZ31 Mg and C27200 Cu–C27200 Cu joints, respectively. Bend tests revealed that similar joints broke with interfacial tear and the dissimilar joints broke with nugget pullout. By conducting pitting corrosion tests on the joints, potentiodynamic polarization curves were developed. The corroded samples were subjected to microstructural evaluation using scanning electron microscopy. The variations in corrosion pit density were also identified.

## 1. Introduction

In manufacturing sector and production industries, joining of materials is very important. A lot of components need to be fabricated according to customized sizes and shapes [1]. Due to the cost of dies and requirement it is not possible to prepare sheets and plates of different sizes [2]. Hence material joining becomes important in industries. From advent of material joining techniques, a lot of methods are being adopted for joining similar and dissimilar materials [3]. In recent times, the demand for joining dissimilar materials has greatly increased [4]. This is because of the demand to utilize the mechanical properties of both the dissimilar materials. In contrast to similar material joining, dissimilar material joining is difficult [5]. This is due to the differences in the mechanical properties and chemical property variations of the dissimilar materials [6]. It is difficult to control the weld region heat flow while using conventional fusion joining methods [7]. Latest solid state joining methods come in handy, while joining dissimilar materials [8].

Friction welding is one such variant which uses the frictional heat from abrasive surfaces for increasing the temperature in the weld region [9]. Friction stir welding uses an external stirring tool for inducing thermo mechanical friction in the joining regions [10]. Friction stir spot welding is a linear variant of the conventional friction stir welding, which can be effectively used for spot joining [11]. There are different techniques employed in friction stir spot welding such as refill friction stir spot welding [12], double friction stir spot welding [13], pin friction spot welding [14] etc. Magnesium and its alloys are being used for structural fabrication in aviation, automotive, pressure vessels and thermal power plants [15]. Magnesium alloys are preferred over other materials due to their light weight and high strength. Magnesium alloys are more durable than other materials. They also exhibit excellent thermal conductivity [16]. Mg alloys were used to prepare reinforced biopolymer composites, as it possesses superior mechanical characteristics and corrosion resistance [17].

Copper and its alloys are corrosion resistant and offer high thermal conductivity [18]. Friction stir spot welding has been used for joining dissimilar materials such as aluminum, magnesium, copper and iron. Depending upon the requirements, the spot joints with dissimilar materials are being preferred.

Corrosion studies are very important in petroleum, mining, high pressure thermal power process plant industries. In these sectors, the materials in usage are continuously subjected to thermal fluctuations [19]. These fluctuations interfere with the normal surface grain structure by inducing cracks and deformations. In addition to thermal fluctuations, the harsh and volatile environment also increases the rate of surface degradation [20]. If left uncared, it may cause huge damage to the parts and replacement would be a costly issue [21]. Hence, for avoiding such damages, corrosion studies are very important.

A lot of investigations have been carried out by researchers for modifying the existing friction stir spot welding technique for improving the quality of the joints [22]. Refill friction stir spot welding variant with seal was used for improving the fatigue behavior of aluminum Al 2024 spot joints [23]. Attempts we made to reduce the keyhole volume by using a pre created hole and then refilling it with multi step refill friction stir spot welding [24]. Generally, friction stir spot welding consists of three steps. The initial step involve plunge of a high speed rotating non consumable tool into the spot joining region. During this step, initial action would be more of mechanical shearing and then the region would be thermo mechanically stirred. The second step is dwelling in which the high speed rotating non‑consumable tool is held in that plunged position for a particular duration of time. This results in softening of the metal in the stir zone. After completion of the dwell duration, the tool is retracted from the plunged position to allow the joint to cool [25]. All these three stages were simulated and modeled using finite element modeling to understand the heat flow through the weld region during the friction stir spot welding process [26]. In the friction stir spot welding region, nano‑particle reinforcement was used for increasing the quality of the joints [27]. During the process of friction stir spot welding usage of primer and sealant helps in increasing the fatigue resistance of the joints [28]. Friction stir spot welded Al joints were subjected to corrosion analysis and its corrosion resistance was identified [29]. Pitting corrosion tests have been used by many researchers for identifying the corrosion characteristics of different metals and metal‑based joints. Stainless steels were subjected to pitting corrosion analysis by using acoustic emission based pitting experiments to identify its corrosion resistance in volatile environments [30]. For protecting the surfaces of metals and metallic joints from environmental corrosion, cyclic potentiodynamic polarization tests using pitting corrosion tests were found to be effective [31]. For identifying the corrosion resistance of 904L steels, pitting corrosion experiments were conducted in sodium bromide environment. Compared to low strength steels, 904L stainless steel exhibited better corrosion resistance [32]. Even though a lot of research has been conducted in corrosion aspects, corrosion studies on dissimilar magnesium and copper joints were not found. The properties of the magnesium‑copper dissimilar joints fabricated using friction stir spot welding have to be studied to understand the behavior of such joints in volatile environment. On evaluating the important literatures and previous investigations, corrosion studies on dissimilar friction stir spot welded Al and Cu joints were not observed. Hence, in this study, an attempt has been made to conduct pitting corrosion investigation on friction stir spot welded dissimilar Al‑Cu joints.

## 2. Materials and methods

In this investigation, Mg based alloys such as AZ 31 Mg and Cu based alloy such as C272500 Cu alloy were chosen as the base materials for conducting friction stir spot welding experiments. The base materials were procured as 2 mm thick rolled sheets. The sheets were then cut to rectangular specimens (100 mm in length and 50 mm in breadth) by using sheet metal cutter. The friction stir spot joints were fabricated in lap configuration. Since Magnesium was softer than Cu, for all joints, AZ31 Mg sheet was placed on the top and C 27200 Cu workpiece was placed in the bottom with an overlap of 30 mm. For conducting friction stir spot welding experiments, a modified heavy type vertical milling machine was used. The cutting tool of the milling machine was replaced by an indigenously prepared friction stir spot welding tool. For preparing the friction stir spot welding tool, HSS material (hardened to 93 HRC) was chosen. The tool was fabricated at 150 mm length; 16 mm shoulder diameter, 6 mm pin diameter and 4 mm pin height. The pin height was made so as to plunge both the top Magnesium sheet and the bottom Cu sheet to the fullest extent in lap configuration. The chemical composition of the base materials were identified by using spark spectrometer. The samples were placed in the spark spectrometer and at different regions sparks were ignited and the chemical composition of the base materials in elemental composition was identified. The mechanical properties of the base materials such as yield strength, ultimate tensile strength and percentage of elongation were identified by conducting tensile tests. For conducting tensile tests, universal testing machine was used. As per ASTM E08 testing standards, the fabricated spot joints were subjected to tensile testing. An incremental load of 1.5 kN/min was given to the joints till fracture. The tensile testing equipment (Make – EIE, DUAL LOAD CELLS – 5KN, 30KN capacity) used for conducting tensile tests is shown in Fig. 1 (a). Vickers hardness testing machine was used for identifying the base material microhardness. As per ASTM E384 standards, Vickers microhardness testing was conducted. A load of 5 kgf was placed on the joint interface for duration of 15 s and the indent was evaluated to identify the Vickers microhardness value. Photograph of Vickers microhardness testing equipment (Make – DHARA, FIE VM‑50) is shown in Fig. 1 (b). As per ASTM E290 bend testing was done. The joints were subjected to bending till fracture of the joints. The maximum bend angle before deformation of the joints was recorded. Photograph of bend testing equipment (Make – CALTECK) used in the experiment is shown in Fig. 1 (c). Photographs of base materials are shown in Fig. 1 (d), Fig. 1 (e) and the friction stir spot welding tool is shown in Fig. 1 (f).

The grain structure of the base materials were studied using scanning electron microscopy. Standard metallographic procedures were used for preparing samples for microstructural evaluation. The surfaces of the base materials were cleaned using acetone and then using emery sheets, the surfaces were polished. Using etchants, the surfaces were etched. Then they were subjected to microscopic evaluation, using scanning electron microscope. Friction stir spot welding experiments were conducted according to the process parameter values indicated as follows. The tool rotational speed was set at 850 rpm; plunge depth was 5 mm and dwell duration was set as 23 s. The friction stir spot welding process parameters were set on studying previous investigations [33,34] and trial experiments. By using the above indicated friction stir spot welding process parameters, experiments were conducted. For performing the experiments, a modified vertical milling machine was used. The friction stir spot welding joints fabricated using AZ31 Mg and C27200 Cu is shown in Fig. 2.

12 joints were fabricated with the same set of process parameters. With the same friction stir spot welding process parameters, similar joints of AZ31 Mg–AZ31 Mg and C 27200 Cu were fabricated. This was done to compare AZ31 Mg–C27200 Cu with AZ 31 Mg–AZ 31 Mg and C27200 Cu–C27200 Cu. As a keyhole is evident in every friction stir spot welded joint, tensile strength could not be calculated. Hence tensile shear failure load was evaluated by using universal testing machine. As per ASTM E 08 standards, an incremental load of 1.5 kN/min was given as tensile load till fracture of the surfaces. At the joint interface, the hardness variations were identified by using Vickers microhardness testing. At the cross section of the joints, using a plunge load of 1.5 kgf for dwell duration of 15 s the indentation was evaluated to identify the Vickers microhardness value. Corrosion studies were conducted by using electro chemical pitting tests. The electrodes were connected to corrosion sensitive transducer and it was evaluated by using Gill software. Potentiodynamic polarization testing was conducted on the dissimilar friction stir spot welded joints such as AZ31Mg–C27200 Cu and the corrosion E<sub>pit</sub> values were compared with the similar friction stir spot welded joints such as AZ31 Mg–AZ31 Mg and C27200 Cu–C27200 Cu joints. As reference electrode, saturated calomel electrode was used. For auxiliary electrode, carbon electrodes were used. Potentiodynamic polarization testing was conducted in aerated NaCl solution. During the entire duration of the pitting corrosion testing, the pH of the solution was maintained as 10, by adding potassium hydroxide. With a scanning potential of 0.166 mV/s the pitting value was measured. The corroded surfaces were evaluated by using scanning electron microscopy.

From tensile shear failure tests, the tensile shear failure load value of dissimilar AZ31 Mg–C27200 Cu joint was recorded at 16.4 N. This value was found to be lower than the tensile shear failure load values of the similar friction stir spot welded joints. The strength of dissimilar joints was found to be approximately 62–69% of the strength of the similar spot joints. Tensile shear failure load value of similar AZ31–AZ31 joint was recorded at 26.3 N and the tensile shear failure load value of similar C27200 Cu–C27200 Cu joint was recorded at 31.6 N. This reduction in tensile characteristics of dissimilar joints was attributed to intermetallic compounds in the joint interfaces [35] and improper diffusion of the dissimilar materials [36]. The bend testing results of the dissimilar and similar joints are shown in Fig. 5 (a). On comparing with the maximum bend angle of similar spot joints, dissimilar spot joints exhibited lower bending angle. The rupture of AZ31 Mg–AZ31 Mg and C27200 Cu–C27200 Cu joints were interfacial break off, during bend testing. On observing the rupture of AZ31 Mg–C27200 Cu was found to be nugget pullout. These ruptures were found to be similar to experimental observations done in previous literatures [37,38]. The variations in Vickers microhardness for the three friction stir spot welded joints have been shown in Fig. 5 (b). Vickers microhardness of the dissimilar friction stir spot welded joints such as AZ31Mg–C27200 joint was found to be 72 HV. It was found to be greater than the joint microhardness of AZ31Mg–AZ31Mg and C27200–C27200 joints respectively. Due to differential grain structure of the joint interface, the weld region microhardness of dissimilar joints increases [39].

**Fig. 5.** (a) Bend test results of the spot joints, (b) Microhardness variations at joint interfaces.

The potentiodynamic polarization graphs of the friction stir spot welded joints (AZ31 Mg–C27200 Cu, AZ31 Mg–AZ31 Mg and C27200 Cu–C27200 Cu) are shown in Fig. 6. From pitting corrosion tests, the E<sub>pit</sub> value of the dissimilar AZ31 Mg–C27200 Cu joint was found to be –259.32 mV. It was lower than the E<sub>pit</sub> values of similar joints. For AZ31 Mg–AZ31 Mg, the E<sub>pit</sub> value was found to be –236.19 mV and the E<sub>pit</sub> value of C 27200 Cu–C 27200 Cu joint was found to be –214.36 mV. When the E<sub>pit</sub> values are inclined towards the positive value, the corrosion resistance is attributed to be greater. SEM images of the corrosion tested joints are shown in Fig. 7. SEM image of AZ31Mg–C27200 Cu dissimilar joint is shown in Fig. 7 (a). It shows relatively dark corroded pits. Due to protrusion of dissimilar material in the frictionally stirred regions, corrosion resistance of dissimilar joints was found to be lower. High dense pits were observed. SEM image of corrosion tested AZ31Mg–AZ31Mg joint is shown in Fig. 7 (b). The corroded regions of similar joints were lesser, compared to the corroded regions of the dissimilar joints. The density of the pits was lower, compared to the corroded regions of the dissimilar joints.

**Fig. 6.** Potentiodynamic polarization curves of corrosion tested (a) AZ31 Mg–C27200 Cu joint, (b) AZ31 Mg–AZ31 Mg joint, (c) C27200 Cu–C27200 Cu joint.

SEM image of corrosion tested C27200 Cu–C27200 Cu joint is shown in Fig. 7 (c). The pits of C27200–C27200 joints were darker than the similar joints of AZ31Mg–AZ31Mg and lighter than AZ31Mg–C27200 joints. It was observed that corrosion micro cracks were found to be more dominant in dissimilar combinations. This was due to the difference in the parent grain structures of AZ31 Mg and C27200 Cu material.

## 4. Conclusions

In this investigation, dissimilar joints using AZ31 Mg and C27200 Cu were successfully fabricated using friction stir spot welding and they were subjected to mechanical and corrosion testing. From this study, the following conclusions were drawn.

i. The mechanical characteristics of the dissimilar friction stir spot welded AZ31 Mg–C27200 Cu joints were compared with similar joints of AZ31 Mg–AZ31 Mg and C27200 Cu–C27200 Cu joints. The strength of the dissimilar joints was found to be 60–68% of the strength of similar joints.  
ii. The surface microhardness of the dissimilar AZ31 Mg–C27200 Cu joints were 22.03% and 35.84% greater than the interface hardness values of similar AZ31 Mg–AZ31 Mg and C27200 Cu–C27200 Cu joints.  
iii. On conducting potentiodynamic polarization testing, the E<sub>pit</sub> values of the dissimilar friction stir spot welding process was found to be more negative than the similar friction stir spot welded combinations. The corroded regions were subjected to microscopic evaluation and the variations in pit density of the joints were identified.
